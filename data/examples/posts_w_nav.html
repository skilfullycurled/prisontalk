A lot has changed since my last note. I am calling the doctor today about putting my mother in the nursing home. No changing minds this time. No more wishful/hopeful thinking. It's down to it. My mother after a day reverted right back to peeing where she sits. This time added verbal that she has no intention on using the toilet. In her words, "This is my house and I can do anything I want in it." She has said this before but this time she seemed to understand what she was saying and why.

This has gotten bigger than me.

For the last year we have talked about going to a nursing home because of her lack of eating. I was always the one vote that kept her home. Thinking that she wouldn't eat anything at the nursing home either. As long as she kept drinking the Boost she would at least stay alive. Then this month I change Doctors and I say to the Doctor, "Is there any drugs that would make her more alert. Be more aware of what she is doing." The Doctor gives me these drugs. And boom she is eating everything put in front of her. She is trying to start conversation with me, calling her friends. Stopped the toilet paper piles. These were all things that we have tried to do the last two years. I sit here wondering why her last doctor didn't offer drugs. Although I asked often they always said nothing or not needed or nothing out there. I'm a little pissed about that. So we get what was are biggest problem somewhat under control and she starts this peeing thing and it brings down the house of cards.

There is nothing I can do to change it. She has decided that there is no reason to use the toilet. It seems all the effort to keep her home was a waste of time.

She is so proud of herself that she is eating. She points it out to me several times a day, "Jim, I ate the whole lunch today." and she still ends up in the nursing home. Every step forward meant nothing in the end.

The worst part is she has nothing medically wrong with her. The new doctor even said he was surprised that at her age and condition that everything works as well as it does. And peeing sends her to the nursing home. Some may say this peeing is a medical problem. I think it is more being lazy. Because if I bark enough she will use the toilet to shut me up.

I got my girl Friday here today and she gave my mother a bath. Paid her 25 bucks with the promise of 35 if my mother gave her a hard time. My mother only complained a few times. First complaint was, "NO!!!" We ignored it and stayed with the plan. Then the water was too hot (it wasn't) Then it was too cold. She was just hoping we would stop. But overall it wasn't bad.

We cleaned the house. It looks like we just moved it. I am not too good in the cleaning department. But, if you walked in here now you would never know that. I had better say. It wasn't garbage. I was clutter. My younger brother was giving me a had time this morning about the mess. I responded with, "Look it's just cluttered and yes it's a mess. But you won't find food just lying around." He took the challenge and searched the house for left over food. The only thing he found was a single ice cream stick with the band of chocolate on the stick. I told him it didn't count.

Anyways, I don't think my mother believes that the nursing home is real. I have used it so many times as a tool to try to make her do something that she thinks it doesn't hold water. In fact she said the other day, "You've been saying nursing home for the last 6 months" So it doesn't hold much weight with her. This time it's real. I don't know how it will go. Will I have to wait or will the Doctor make a call and then call me the same day and say "Bring her to <name here> nursing home at 2:00 today." I kind of think she will go very soon because the peeing has caused medical problems. A problem they can't ignore. So if they do this same day. I will be in for a hell of a day. She won't understand that the nursing home is real. She will be yelling and screaming the whole way with a lot of crying in between. And what happens if the real trip to the nursing home she changes her stand on the peeing and promises to use the toilet. Up until putting her in the car the nursing home wasn't real. I still think the peeing is a choice she has made. For a number of reasons 1.) Lazy 2.) Her way of not allowing me control. 3.) That she holds the power to do anything she wants. I think she would stop once she sees she doesn't have the power. That I have the control. And the nursing home is a real thing. And if that is the case the nursing home is not the correct option.

The nursing home experience will be a whole different world for her. No smoking which is very important to her. Being man handled by people she doesn't know. Stuff that should be done but she is not used to being done. I wonder how many times she will yell out ,"Jim, can I have ...." and no one responds and when someone does, it's not me. When she realizes that Jim isn't there at all.

On the other hand she will be around other people her own age and someone hopefully as nutty as she is to talk to. Plus, she will have better care than I could ever do. Also, be around staff 24/7 that can recognize problems that I would miss at home. Plus if a problem does happen she is with people that can deal with it at the time it happens. Where here I would have to hang out and wait for the fire department to show up. These are big plus's for her. But from her point of view she would focus on the negitives.

As for me. I have no idea what I am going to do. It will be just me and the hairy beast. I feel quite lost right now. I was thinking this morning I might have to bring the hairy beast back to the pound. He is such a nice dog I think someone would take him home quickly. However, he was at the pound for 6 months before I got him. I may have to live in my car. I don't have a dime to my name. Once my mother enters the nursing home her income stops and goes directly to the nursing home. I don't know how fast the state will act on taking my mothers assets. If they are slow as most government things I will have enough time to get a job and save a bit to get my own place. If they act quick I will be stuck out in the cold. Plus, I don't think I want to stay in NY. I hate snow. More exact, I don't like shoveling snow. Maybe move west and live in a teepee. Me and Shaddy. Live like the unabomber in a shack in the woods in the middle of nowhere. Living on sneals and rabbits.

Wow, you guys, I usually don't look up here, but it seems there's a whole nother world going on!

Oh, Jimbo, I remember it well! After my mother actually went in the nursing home for good (she'd been there for just a short time after a hospitalization a few years before), I found myself going over there to help her with every meal for a while. But, she did improve both physically and emotionally, once there, and made friends with both staff and other residents. For awhile, it was a full life for her, and I actually came to regret not having put her in sooner, because she brightened up with all the new relationships and stimulation (very few friends were ever visiting, by that time, after the many years she'd been virtually home-bound). She stayed close to 4 years, before she died, and the situation did not stay as desirable as the years went by -- it was a profit-making nursing home, and as time passed, there were gradually fewer activities and fewer non-care support staff. Luckily, the wonderful regular CNA stayed till the end of her life, which made just a huge difference in the quality of her life. We did find, as others have mentioned, that visiting both frequently, and at unpredictable times periodically, helped to keep the staff more concerned about her. And, I like to think that my making friends with staff also helped -- most (though maybe not all?) had good intentions, and feeling closer to her encouraged this, and I think resulted in better treatment. (She would periodically have psychotic episodes that sometimes lasted for weeks, and, while in them, she was a real challenge to deal with, though most of the time she was a real dear.)

Jimbo, I know that it was hard for you to come to this decision. I'm sorry that others had such a hard time with their loved ones nursing homes. But, there are some really good ones out there.

LS and Thumper, you are to be commended for your caring services to your respective nursing homes. It's a tough job. ESPECIALLY when you care. I have three members in my immediate family that were CNA's. All three eventually burned out. It's fairly common for that to happen. It's backbreaking, heartbreaking, work.

I'll never forget the day my mother decided to retire. She cried her eyes out. Because, even though our local nursing home is considered the best in the state of Michigan, there are always a few bad people in the bunch. She had always kept an eye on the ones that were a little less diligent. She was worried that others wouldn't keep such a close eye out. She felt better when, on her last day, she saw and heard another CNA chew another ones butt for not changing someones Depends in a timely manner.
