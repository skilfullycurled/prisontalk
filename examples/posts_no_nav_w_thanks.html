Impact on family members
I wasnt sure where to post this or even if I should.
I just wanted to share something that happened yesterday with my son.

His car is not working. (his fault grrrrrrrrr)
Anyway...we have been juggling cars. Hub takes mine usually, and I drive a truck. Since son's car is dead, we try to figure out how to get each of us to work and home.

Since I work the closest to home, I usually get a ride from my son in the morning (work at 7:30am) and son drives home and then goes to work at 11am (usually)
So yesterday we did this. I was not sure if he would get off in time to pick me up so I hitched a ride home with a coworker who lives in town, and was leaving the same time I was.

So as we are waiting to turn onto the street that connects to our street....here comes son. So now he's ahead of us and we are behind him.
Son drives into our court. He turns around at the end and drives out of the court. Coworker says....isnt that your truck? I say yes. Wonder whats going on? Maybe he forgot to pick up the dog from day care....

Anyway, I get inside... and coworker drives off. I then see son driving back in the court.

I asked him what happened? Did you see me getting a ride? He said, no but I saw that truck following me into the court and I didnt know who it was......so I didnt want to drive home so I drove past so whomever it was didnt know where I lived.

He was afraid it might be some vigilante or something.

It sort of shocked me he would do that. I understand it, and its a good idea. But dang.

Any time a car went past the house I was looking out the window to see if it was the police! To this day (3 years later) I still get worried when I see a police car...and the worst thing I've ever done was get a DWI back in my college days!
Paranoia is only natural. Just need to tell your son this. Maybe communication is the key here. Can't imagine a child who doesn't have a cell phone (LOL)! Just keep him informed of what's going on when plans change! By the way, I don't look out the window nearly as much now...it gets better!
