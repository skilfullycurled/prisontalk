Which State Makes Being A Sex Offender Most Difficult
I would like to open a discussion about which state everyone thinks has the most restrictions and regulations with regards to classifying, supervising and sentencing sex offenders.
You see someone mention it lightly every once in awhile. "Which state would I be able to live the fullest life in?" Or, "Which state do I avoid?"
Even though there have been and continue to be sweeping changes, new laws, and more stupidity to attempt to "regulate" all states the same it is obvious that every state and/or communities within that state have HUGE variances.
So for this one Which state would you advise your beloved Sex Offender to avoid?
and, why?
Then find the other thread started to stake your claim in which state you define as being the least restrictive and liveable with the most sweeping reform to alleviate its SO's from unconstitutional laws and lets them live like humans.
Please use laws, rumors, discrepancies in perception and court cases to make your claim. Let's talk about it and figure it out!

I think asking whether Georgia is better than Texas for sex offenders is a little like asking whether plague is better than polio for a patient. Both choices roundly suck. I hate to say that, since I am originally from Texas, and my family and friends still live there. I'd move back if I thought it wouldn't make my situation worse.

As an aside, you have to say there are a couple of positive aspects in the move by several states to put the vast majority of offenders on the lifetime registration list. First, it greatly accelerates the pace at which the list is becoming next to useless. Second, when registration was first getting started, some states had a rule that your time was ten years from the date of first registration IN THAT STATE. Register for nine years in State A, follow all the rules, move to State B, and start over with ten years to go. It's stupid, but it was designed to keep sex offenders from finding a more lenient state to which to move so they could plan their next dastardly deeds in peace...or something to that effect. With lifetime registration, WHO CARES when the clock starts?

I'd put Virginia on the "bad" list. I'm not sure it's the worst, but the legislators are trying. A shout out and a word of thanks to RSOL of Virginia who is at least making them THINK about the laws they are trying to pass. Keep up the good work.
